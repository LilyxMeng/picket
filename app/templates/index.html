About 1.3 billion tons of waste go to landfills every year. As this waste decomposes, methane gas is released into the atmosphere, while other chemical byproducts leak into nearby water sources and contaminate them. However, almost 80% of waste in landfills can be recycled. To help people recycle more materials, we created Picket.

Idea

Picket is a web app that determines what category of trash an object is. Once a file of an image is uploaded, Picket will scan it and determine which category of trash the image best fits into. There are six categories: e-waste, glass, metal, paper, plastic, or organic.

E-Waste



To be recycled, e-waste (electronic waste) is typically disassembled, categorized and cleaned, then shredded mechanically for further sorting.

Electronics are full of harmful chemicals and substances such as toxic metals and pollutants. These substances can contaminate the environment.

Glass



To be recycled, glass is crushed, blended, and melted together with sand and other starting materials. An advantage of glass is that it can be recycled endlessly.

While glass does not release any dangerous materials during its breakdown process, broken glass is a physical hazard in the environment.

Metal



To be recycled, metals are processed, shredded, and melted in furnaces to be produced into different products.

Metals left in the environment can cause water contamination in surface and ground waters due to their toxic nature.

Paper



To be recycled, paper is mixed with chemicals to be broken down. It is heated, which breaks it down into strands of cellulose. Once de-inked and mixed with water, it can be made new.

Paper might decompose through anaerobic processes releasing methane, or be burned at a landfill, releasing greenhouse gases into the environment.

Plastic



To be recycled, plastics are shredded into flakes, which are then soaked in hot water. Then, the plastic is melted to produce granules, and finally molded into a new product.

Plastic can release toxic chemicals, its debris can be quite harmful, and plastic decomposes very slowly in nature.

We are all highschool students who deeply care about the issue of waste. The amount of waste we produce as humans is alarming. As people who wanted to combat the issue, we created Picket to engage our community in our effort to be more aware of our environment.
